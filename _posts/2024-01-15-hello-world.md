Every developer eventually builds their own website.
This is mine — a small patch of the internet where I can post about things I'm working on,
experiments that didn't quite pan out, and the occasional overly-detailed writeup on something
that probably didn't need a writeup.

## Why Jekyll?

Simple, fast, and it lives in a git repo. No databases to babysit, no CMS to update.
Just markdown files and a static site generator doing what it's good at.

The theme is custom — dark with a slightly whimsical feel because I spend too much time
staring at dark terminals and might as well make them look interesting.

## What to expect here

Mostly software things:

- Rust experiments and notes on the borrow checker defeating me
- Python scripts that ballooned into something more elaborate
- Hardware/embedded projects
- Things I learned the hard way so you don't have to
